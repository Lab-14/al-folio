Presently, I am working as a research scientist (post-doctoral researcher) in Living Analytics Research Centre (LARC) at School of Computing and Information Systems, Singapore Management University under supervision of Prof. Archan Misra.

My research is primarily focused in the areas of the Embedded Sensing, Ubiquitous Computing, Low-power System Design, and Internet-of-Things (IoT).

Prior to LARC, I have received my Ph.D. training from IIIT-Delhi under wonderful supervision of Dr. Shobha Sundar Ram (Department of Electronics Engineering) and Dr. Amarjeet Singh (Joint faculty - Department of Computer Science and Electronics Engineering) with a specialization in Embedded Sensing for Energy Sustainability Applications.

During my doctoral studies, I had a chance to work as a visiting researcher with Prof. Shwetak Narain Patel (University of Washington, Seattle, USA) and Sidhant Gupta (Microsoft Research, Redmond) at Ubicomp Lab in Paul G. Allen Centre for Computer Science and Engineering, University of Washington, Seattle, WA (USA) from 2016-2018.

After completing my doctoral research, I have also spent some time consulting with a few technology startups i.e. Aerogram India Pvt. Ltd. focusing on Distributed Air Quality Monitoring and Child Health Imprints Pte. Ltd. which leverages the power of IoT for neonatal health monitoring in NICU's. I have also served a short stint at IIT Kanpur as a visiting faculty for E-ICT Academy in 2019 Summer.

Before joining IIIT-Delhi, I have worked with Hewlett-Packard Educational services (Noida), WiFinity Tech (Bangalore) and Indrion Technologies (Bangalore) as Embedded Design Engineer where we designed and deployed Wireless Sensor Networks for Smart Energy applications. I received my B.Tech (with Hons.) in Electronics and Communication Engineering in 2011 from Maharishi Dayanand University, Rohtak .

research interests

Embedded Sensing, Low-power System Design, Ubiquitous Computing, Internet of Things (IoT), Energy Harvesting (Self-powered Systems), and Energy Sustainability
